1. **Tripathi, V. (2025), `Quantified Noun Phrases in Hindi: A Predicate Logic-Based Formalization'.**
Research Area: Derived from L$_{2H}$ fragment, this paper focuses on how generalized quantifiers interact with predicates in Hindi, exploring scope ambiguities and NP-predicate relationships.

2. **Tripathi, V. (2025), `Type-Theoretic Analysis of Verbal Constructions in Hindi: Light Verbs and Auxiliaries'.**
Research Area: This paper elaborates on the L$_{3H}$ fragment using type theory to formalize Hindi verb phrases, focusing on light verbs, argument structures, and auxiliaries.
    
3. **Tripathi, V. (2025), `Temporal Logic and Aspectual Interpretation in Hindi: Analyzing Near and Far Perfection'.**
Research Area: This paper elaborates on the L$_{4H}$ fragment, detailing how temporal logic is used to formalize tense and aspect in Hindi sentences, particularly focusing on perfective and progressive aspects.
    
4. **Tripathi, V. and Rathod D. (2025), `Development of a Hindi Syntax Tree Analyzer: The Hindi Tree'.**
Research Area: This paper focuses on the tool developed, \textit{The Hindi Tree (THT)}, which generates syntax trees for Hindi sentences. It describes the parser's architecture, its underlying grammar, and applications in linguistic analysis. It reports performance metrics of THT using a customised corpus.

5. **Tripathi, V. (2025), `Lambda Abstraction and Compositional Semantics in Hindi: A Formal Approach'.**
Research Area: This article aims to formalize the application of \textit{lambda calculus} in representing the semantics of Hindi sentences. In addition to formalizing the fundamental syntactic categories of Hindi, it addresses the treatment of complex verb structures, quantification, and variable binding.

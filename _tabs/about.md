My research approach has been interdisciplinary learning of selected topics of **Maths** (Discreet), **Languages** (such as Hindi), and **Computer Science** (Python). My primary interest is exploring how formal and experimental methods can enable a machine to learn a natural language, e.g. Hindi. My specific research topics in language have included syntax, semantics, logic, modality, case markers, and negative polarity items.

My dissertation, **A Modern Grammar of Hindi: A Formal Approach**, develops a formal method for Hindi, utilizing propositional, predicate, type, and temporal logic. This grammar has been implemented in a Python-based parsing tool (whose offline version was released on May 24, and I am working to launch its web version by July,25). I have worked on transitive verbs, intransitive verbs, quantified constructions, and time-based expressions.

I received my **PhD in Applied Linguistics** from the Indian Institute of Technology (BHU) Varanasi in September 2025. Before IIT(BHU), I studied at the Indian Institute of Technology Bombay and Technische Universitat Dresen as a DAAD Scholar in 2013. I completed my engineering in 2013.



I am experienced in leveraging technological frameworks to provide a robust language-based solution. I have developed minor tools using Python libraries such as *scikit, nltk,* etc., for higher-level modelling and analysis. I am experienced in building *machine learning models*, identifying key architecture parameters, and implementing vectorized *neural networks* in language applications.
